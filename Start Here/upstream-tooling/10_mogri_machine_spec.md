## This document defines mogri as a structural property of state-transition systems.

Mogri is the structure of state transitions under constraint, requiring: low identity coupling, non-validating reachability, reversible connectivity (cost symmetry), and bounded optionality.

Identity coupling is the control variable: it adds identity_penalty to transitions; mogri survives only while identity_penalty ≪ base_cost, where cost denotes transition effort, probability, or resource.

Non-validating reachability allows entry into states without identity gatekeeping; reversible connectivity requires return cost ≈ forward cost; bounded optionality maintains multiple distinct, viable, reversibly connected paths.

Mogri collapses when identity coupling rises, causing gated access, cost asymmetry: forward cost ≪ return cost, or collapse of viable alternatives, even if surface behavior appears adaptive.

Progmogriess emerges only when mogri is sustained, allowing difference to be entered and reintegrated, expanding state space and tolerance for unresolved coexistence.
