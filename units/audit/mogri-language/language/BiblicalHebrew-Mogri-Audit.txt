CORE QUESTION
-------------
Does Biblical Hebrew provide a neutral carrier for persistence-through-change?

CANDIDATE TERMS
---------------
- היה (hāyāh)
- נהיה (nihyāh)
- הפך (hāfakh)

WHY THEY FAIL
-------------
- hāyāh implies being or coming-to-be
- nihyāh implies becoming
- hāfakh implies reversal or transformation
- strong theological loading throughout

COMMON WORKAROUNDS USED INSTEAD
-------------------------------
- narrative sequencing
- verb aspect
- parallelism
- divine framing

COST OF WORKAROUND
------------------
Medium (contextual, narrative load)

VERDICT
-------
Mogri present? no

NOTES
-----
Change is framed theologically and narratively rather than abstractly.
Neutral lexicalisation is structurally disfavoured.
