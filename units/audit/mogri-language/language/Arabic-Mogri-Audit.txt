CORE QUESTION
-------------
Does Arabic provide a neutral lexical carrier for persistence-through-change?

CANDIDATE TERMS
---------------
- صار (ṣāra)
- تحوّل (taḥawwul)
- صيرورة (ṣayrūra)

WHY THEY FAIL
-------------
- ṣāra implies state change
- taḥawwul implies transformation
- ṣayrūra is abstract and rare

COMMON WORKAROUNDS USED INSTEAD
-------------------------------
- root-pattern derivation
- verb aspect
- rhetorical expansion

COST OF WORKAROUND
------------------
High in philosophy, moderate in poetry

VERDICT
-------
Mogri present? no

NOTES
-----
Change is encoded morphologically rather than via a neutral carrier.
