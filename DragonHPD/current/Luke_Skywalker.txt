what actor roles are they playing?

let’s place the lanterns properly, because you’ve braided two moments on purpose.

After Ben is lost (Episode IV):
Luke is rescued emotionally by the crew, not mystically.
Han gets him off Tatooine. Leia gives him purpose.
But no one teaches him yet. He’s carrying an ember with no hand around it.

Ben’s role (Alec Guinness) doesn’t end as a teacher - it converts into direction without intervention.
He becomes the voice, not the guide. Nudge, not hand-holding.
“Trust yourself.” That’s it. No curriculum.

After the tauntaun (Episode V):
Now we’re in the ice and the gut and the near-death.

Luke is rescued physically by Han Solo (Harrison Ford), not a Jedi.
Important: survival first, mysticism later.
The Force does not save him here - a smuggler does.

While he’s freezing, Ben appears again - but notice the shift.
He does not teach.
He redirects.

He names the next teacher.

Yoda (Frank Oz, body; puppeteers unseen, voice the anchor)
Role: not mentor-as-authority, but mentor-as-obstacle.
He teaches by frustrating Luke’s expectations of teaching.

How Luke gets there:
No prophecy taxi.
No Force Uber.

He follows coordinates from R2-D2 - the archivist, not the hero.
His steed becomes the X-wing, then his own body.
Dagobah strips him of spectacle: no crowd, no praise, no clean ground.

So the chain is:

Ben (Alec Guinness): awakener → disembodied compass

Han (Harrison Ford): rescuer → keeps Luke alive

R2-D2: map → continuity of knowledge

Yoda (Frank Oz): teacher → dismantler of ego

Dagobah: steed → environment as instructor

No one “raises” Luke in a straight line.
Each figure hands him off sideways.

That’s the pattern:
rescue → redirection → relocation → resistance → learning

Wins never arrive head-on.
