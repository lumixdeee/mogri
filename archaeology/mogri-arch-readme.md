# Mogri for Archaeology

This directory applies mogri terminology to archaeology, history, pre-literate evidence, and paleo interpretation. The aim is not to replace existing archaeological terms such as taphonomy, formation processes, equifinality, stratigraphy, residue analysis, or confidence ranking. The aim is to provide a short handle for a repeated problem: traces survive, labels arrive, and the unknowns that matter are often still active.

Mogri means the unknowns that matter. Demogrify means to know, reveal, expose, or resolve some or all of those unknowns. Mogrification is the making, preserving, restoring, or recognition of unknowns that matter. It may be intentional, accidental, or academic. In the narrower operational sense, to mogrify is to occult knowns that matter. In academic use, mogrification can remove certainty that evidence has not earned.

The basic stage example is simple. A flying actor is supported by hidden wires. The flight matters, and the wire mechanism matters, but seeing the wires damages the effect. The wires are mogrified. If an audience member sees them, then for that person the mechanism of flight has been demogrified.

In archaeology, the same logic helps with pits, graves, bones, walls, skull fragments, postholes, fills, animal remains, route systems, residues, and missing timber. Some unknowns are just ordinary absence. Others change the model if they become known. Those are mogri.

## Files

- `mogri_for_archaeology.txt`: introduces the vocabulary for archaeological use.
- `false_demogrification_in_archaeology.txt`: explains the error where labels behave as though unknowns have been resolved before the evidence supports that state.
- `gobekli_tepe_as_mogri_case_study.txt`: applies the method to Gobekli Tepe, using ranked roles rather than one hard label.
- `examples/`: optional working examples, drafts, or case files.

## Working rule

Start with the object. State what is physically known. Name the mogri. Ask how that mogri formed: ancient concealment, accidental loss, later disturbance, or modern overclaim. Demogrify only what the evidence resolves. Rank the remaining roles. State what would demogrify the next unknown.

## Why this matters

Archaeology often works where written explanation is missing or later. A feature may be ritual, practical, domestic, symbolic, defensive, storage-related, reused, disturbed, or several of those at once. Mogri language helps keep roles visible without pretending they are all equally supported.

The point is not to make the past vague. The point is to stop a useful label from becoming a lock. A claim can be strong, weak, speculative, or wrong. Mogri helps name what still matters while the next test, map, trench, tray, or fragment changes the model.

## Short glossary

Mogri: the unknowns that matter.

Mogrification: the making, preserving, restoring, or recognition of unknowns that matter. This may be intentional, accidental, or academic.

Mogrify: to produce, preserve, restore, or recognize mogri. In the narrower operational sense, to occult knowns that matter.

Demogrify: to know, reveal, expose, or resolve some or all of the unknowns that matter.

Partial demogrification: evidence resolves one important unknown while others remain.

False demogrification: a claim behaves as though unknowns that matter are resolved when they are not.

Remogrify: to restore mogri to a claim after critique, new evidence, or removal of unsupported certainty.

Mogri load: the amount and importance of unresolved unknowns carried by a feature, object, deposit, or model.

Mogri lock: a rule that stops one known role from swallowing other live roles.
